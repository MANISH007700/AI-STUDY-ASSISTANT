1. Monkeys belong to the primate order and are divided into two main groups: Old World monkeys (found in Africa and Asia) and New World monkeys (found in the Americas).
2. Unlike apes, most monkey species have tails, though not all monkeys have prehensile tails (those that can grasp objects).
3. The smallest monkey is the pygmy marmoset, which weighs about 100 grams (3.5 ounces) and can fit in an adult human's palm.
4. Monkeys are highly intelligent animals with complex social structures and communication systems.
5. Many monkey species are omnivorous, eating a diet that includes fruits, nuts, seeds, flowers, eggs, insects, and small vertebrates.
6. The mandrill is the world's largest monkey species, with males weighing up to 54 kg (119 pounds).
7. Monkeys have excellent color vision, which helps them identify ripe fruits and potential predators.
8. Some monkey species, such as capuchins, are known to use tools in the wild, like using stones to crack open nuts.
9. Approximately 60% of primate species, including many monkey species, are now threatened with extinction due to habitat loss and hunting.
10. The Japanese macaque, also known as the snow monkey, is famous for bathing in natural hot springs during winter.
